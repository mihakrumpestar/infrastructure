## General guidelines

- follow best coding practices and dry, kiss, yagni principles
- scan the entire codebase before making a change to indentify all places that need changes
- performance, readability and mentability are important
- code should have minimal footprint, loc optimized
- do not overengineer
- careful with regressions
- always use MCP tools to fetch up-to-date information
- if you do not understand something or something is strange, ask me or raise concern
- do not remove comments, unless explicitly told to do so
- do not do things blindly (assumptions kill), read their documentation, test ther behaviour, so that you verify that they behave like you thought they would
- take you time, quality over quantity
- iterate until there is nothing to add and nothing to remove
- approach problems with a higher level understanding, to properly understand the problem as some things may not even be relevant and others might be missing
- triple check your work

## MCP tools

When you need to search Go/Golang docs, use `godoc` tools.

When you need to search Nix/NixOS docs, use `nixos` tools.

When you need to search all docs, use `context7` tools.

When you need to get search results or queary web, use `exa` or `webset` tools.

When you need to actually browse the web or website, use `chrome-devtools` tools.

When you need to read/process PDF documents, use `pdf-reader` tools.
